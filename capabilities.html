Capabilities

Explore all of the equipment that Maker Central has to offer.

Jump to: Laser Cutters, 3D Printers, Fabrication, Vinyl Cutter, or Microelectronics.

Laser Cutters

The Epilog Fusion 40 and FSLaser Hobby Laser are available for use in the Maker Space. To operate this equipment, you will have to attend a Maker 101 Orientation session. If you are experienced with any of this equipment and feel that you do not need the training -- contact us to discuss alternate training options.

FSLaser Cutter

We have an FSLaser Hobby laser ENGRAVER. Although the FSLaser has the settings to cut things, it is very very bad at it. For this reason, ONLY USE THE FSLASER FOR RASTER ENGRAVING. The FSLaser should be calibrated and functional.

Training documents

Epilog Laser Cutter

Cut acrylic, wood, and other materials rapidly and with precision. If you’re making something that can be decomposed into interlocking flat pieces then this is your go-to machine.

Training documents

3D Printers

The Solidoodle and Stratasys 3D printers are available for use in the Maker Space. To operate this equipment, you will have to attend a Maker 101 Orientation session. If you are experienced with any of this equipment and feel that you do not need the training -- contact us to discuss alternate training options.

Solidoodle 3D Printer

Entry level ABS 3D printer. These are inexpensive enough to buy one for your garage (but you should play with one here first). Create objects with a .2 mm layer height in semi-flexible ABS plastic.

Training documents

Stratasys 3D Printer

High end, dual extrusion 3D printer. Consistent results below .1 mm layer height with the convenience of soluble support material.

Training documents

Fabrication

The fabrication capabilities in Maker Central space include a drill press, lathe, band saw, and a Tormach PCNC 70. To operate this equipment, you will have to attend a Maker 101 Orientation session. If you are experienced with any of this equipment and feel that you do not need the training -- contact us to discuss alternate training options.

Tormach PCNC 770

Powerful and precise CNC Mill. Requires expertise and extensive training to use but this machine is capable of milling everything from printed circuit boards to engine blocks.

Training documents

Drill Press

We have two. We have a small drill press that’s great if you just want a nice perpendicular hole in roughly the right place. We also have a larger drill press with a moveable vice for more precise work.

Training documents

Mini-Lathe

Small lathe that is appropriate for turning down aluminum stock. This is a good place to get started.

Training documents

Mega-Lathe

Heavy duty industrial machinery. This isn’t operational yet but will be a workhorse once we get it online.

Training documents

Vinyl Cutter

Cut self-adhesive vinyl stickers. Stickers? You laugh? Once you can create your own official looking signs, you basically rule the world. Maker Central is equipped with a Copam 4500 Vinyl Cutter. In order to use the cutter, makers must attend a safety and basic usage (SBU) training. You must attend our Vinyl Cutting 101 course and perform the necessary steps in the SBU check-out before using the cutter without supervision.

Microelectronics

Arduino, Raspberry Pi and Beagle Bone are a few of the microelectronics capabilities available in the Maker space.
